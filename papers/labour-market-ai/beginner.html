Labour Market Matching Efficiency in the Age of AI

A plain-language version of the paper.

What is the paper about?

The paper is about how well workers and jobs are matched to each other, and whether AI makes that process better or worse.

Why does it matter?

Better matching can reduce unemployment, improve productivity, and help workers find roles that fit their skills more closely.

Where AI fits in

AI can improve search, filtering, screening, and sorting, which may help workers and firms find each other faster and more accurately.

Main takeaway

The effect of AI depends on whether it reduces frictions in the labour market faster than it creates new ones.
